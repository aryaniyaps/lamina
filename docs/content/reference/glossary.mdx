# Glossary

## Actor

A role that interacts with the product — e.g. guest, host, admin. Actors have goals, permissions, and distinct flows. Defined during `/lamina-init` and expanded in `/lamina-design`.

## Contract

The complete product behavior specification for a run. Composed of `run.yaml` (metadata) and `implement.md` (build handoff). The contract is what your agent implements and Lamina verifies against.

## Domain charter

Output of `/lamina-init`. Scopes the product domain — entities, actors, and boundaries — before any flow design begins.

## Domain

The problem space you're designing for — e.g. "exam hall ticket system" or "short-term rental marketplace." Broader than a single feature or flow.

## Finding

A product behavior gap discovered during `/lamina-verify`. Documented in `fix.md` with expected vs observed behavior and a suggested fix.

## Invariant

A rule that must always hold in the product — e.g. "a cancelled booking cannot also be confirmed" or "payment requires an active hold." Violations are caught during verification.

## Persona walk

A verification pass where a subagent navigates your live application as a specific actor, checking flows and invariants against the contract.

## ready_to_build

Contract status indicating the design is complete enough for your agent to implement. Set by `/lamina-design` when actors, states, flows, and edge cases are specified.

## Run

A single design-verify cycle identified by a short hash (e.g. `a3f2`). All artifacts for one scope live in `.lamina/runs/<id>/`.

## State

A condition an entity can be in — e.g. `pending`, `confirmed`, `cancelled`. States have defined transitions and may have recovery paths when transitions fail.

## Workflow

A sequence of steps an actor follows to accomplish a goal — e.g. guest checkout: search → hold → pay → confirm. Workflows include primary paths, alternate paths, and error recovery.
