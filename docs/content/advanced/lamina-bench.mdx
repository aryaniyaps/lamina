# LaminaBench

LaminaBench v2 tests the actual Lamina thesis: can an agent turn an incomplete idea into a more coherent product contract, and does that contract improve later implementation?

<Callout type="warning">
No v2 publication result exists yet. The development protocol and claim gates are implemented; held-out packages, blinded ratings, confirmatory runs, and the human iteration study must finish before marketing claims are made.
</Callout>

## Linked studies

| Study | Primary question | Claim gate |
|---|---|---|
| Contract quality | Is the frozen product graph materially better? | Blinded human quality, task-level effect, omission and failure guardrails |
| Fresh-builder transfer | Can a new agent build a better product from the frozen native contract? | Downstream quality and critical-omission reduction |
| Agent iteration efficiency | Does the full loop reach trusted behavior with less time or rework? | Efficiency gain with quality non-inferiority |
| Human iteration study | Do real builders spend less active effort shaping a trusted contract? | Powered randomized crossover; separate from agent wall time |

Passing a contract-quality benchmark does not automatically prove faster human iteration. The claim-gate tool exposes each claim separately and reports human iteration speed as `not_tested` until the crossover study passes.

## Arms and tracks

| Arm | Contract method |
|---|---|
| `raw` | Best free-form contract, no required reviewers |
| `structured` | Strong neutral graph schema plus three isolated generic critics |
| `lamina` | Contract v2 plus isolated, bounded persona perspectives |

The autonomous track continues with classified assumptions. The oracle track permits at most three high-leverage questions through a deterministic topic-indexed broker. Tracks are analyzed separately.

Lamina versus raw estimates ecological value. Lamina versus the strong structured arm estimates method value without credit for schema compliance, Lamina terminology, or persona artifacts themselves.

## Analysis unit and size

The task—not a repeat—is the independent unit. Publication uses 12 held-out tasks, two tracks, three arms, two repeats, and four model cohorts: 144 primary-cohort trial cells and 576 cells across confirmatory plus replication cohorts. Each cell also runs its assigned isolated reviewers and a fresh transfer builder. Repeats estimate within-task model variance; they never inflate the task sample size.

## Development commands

```bash
npm run bench:v2:preflight
npm run bench:v2:matrix -- --mode development --cohort openai-gpt-5.6-sol
npm run bench:v2:run -- --mode development --cohort openai-gpt-5.6-sol --limit 1
```

The bundled host runner is development-only. Publication matrices are exported after corpus, model, runtime, scoring, and skill freezes, then executed by an external custodian that prevents access to goldens, oracle facts, other briefs, and previous cells.

## Claim discipline

- Human ratings are primary; frozen model ratings are sensitivity evidence.
- Score coherent alternatives and propagated consequences, not verbosity or match to an unstated author preference.
- Report task medians and paired effects for every task.
- Calibrate the minimum meaningful effect before publication scoring.
- Do not market a tiny point lift merely because it is positive.
