# Human iteration validation

Run a pilot with at least 12 product-minded engineers, technical founders, or design engineers who regularly build with coding agents. Use the paired variance from that pilot and the frozen minimum meaningful reduction to power the confirmatory randomized crossover at 90% power and two-sided 5% alpha, with a floor of 24 completed participants. Each participant shapes two matched incomplete ideas, one with Lamina and one with their normal agent workflow. Counterbalance order and task assignment.

Stop when the participant declares the contract implementation-ready. Capture active human minutes, corrective turns, manually introduced product rules, time to accepted contract, confidence, perceived workload, and the frozen contract. Blindly rate both contracts with the benchmark rubric; send a preregistered subset to fresh builders.

Preregister exclusions, the paired estimator, the time SESOI, the quality non-inferiority margin, and missing-session handling before confirmatory recruitment. Claim reduced human iteration effort only when active effort falls materially and blinded contract plus downstream quality are non-inferior or better. Customer interviews motivate this study but are not outcome evidence.
