# Design Process

## When to load

- human-centered design workflow
- diverge-converge product development
- creative teamwork practices
- full product development
- problem to solution journey

## Decision frameworks

- **Human-Centered Design (HCD) Process**: Iterative cycle ensuring products meet human needs, are understandable, usable, and enjoyable. - When to use: Any product development. - How: Observe people in natural environment; generate alternatives; prototype; test; repeat.
- **Double-Diamond Diverge-Converge Model**:
- **Four Activities of HCD**:
- **Small, Focused Teams**: Compact interdisciplinary groups with shared ownership of design outcomes.
- **Thinking Better, Together**: Brainstorming, sketching, design studios for generating and evaluating alternatives.
- **Working across Design Disciplines**: Interaction and visual designers as partners from framework stage.
- **The Extended Team**: Engaging PMs, engineers, QA, and business stakeholders in the design process.
- **Establishing a Creative Culture**: Environment where exploration, critique, and iteration are safe.
- **Identifying Skill Levels in Designers**: Match tasks to junior, intermediate, senior capability.
- **Identifying Skill Levels in Designers**: Junior, intermediate, senior—assign work appropriately and mentor deliberately. - Use when staffing design projects. - How: Seniors lead framework and critique; juniors execute refinement with review; intermediates own features end-to-end.

## Checklists

1. HCD: observe, ideate, prototype, test—iterate continuously.
2. Double-diamond: diverge-converge on problem, then on solution.
3. Never solve the given problem without discovery first.
4. Requirements from observation beat requirements from questionnaires alone.
5. Prototyping and testing are mandatory, not optional polish.
6. Design thinking solves right problem with human needs central.
7. Iteration looks slow to managers but prevents expensive wrong solutions.
1. Small, focused interdisciplinary teams outperform large siloed groups.
2. Thinking together beats solo heroics.
3. Interaction and visual design are partners from framework stage onward.
4. Extended team participation builds buy-in and surfaces constraints early.
5. Creative culture and collaboration skill matter as much as individual craft.

## Heuristics

- **Solve the correct problem**: Brilliant solution to wrong problem worse than none.
- **Never accept problem statement as given**: Treat as suggestion; dig for root issues.
- **Iterative expansion**: Managers see designers "going backward"—it's discovery.
- **Activity-centered vs. human-centered**: Tension between task efficiency and human experience.
- **Design thinking hallmark**: Not designer monopoly—innovators across fields use it.
- Think**diverge before converge**twice—once on problem, once on solution.
- Use consulting rule:**never solve the problem you're asked to solve**—first.
- Treat prototypes as**questions**, not answers—cheap learning vehicles.
- **Collaboration Is the Key**: Speed and quality increase when developers and designers work together fully.
- **Design leadership**: Senior designers orchestrate process; don't hoard creative decisions.
- **Creative culture**: Psychological safety to propose, critique, and abandon bad ideas early.
- Design teams are**jazz ensembles**—individual skill in service of collective output.
- Silos are the**failure mode**—handoffs lose context and intent.
- Critique the**design against personas**, not the designer's taste.
- **Decision filter**: Ask whether this finding changes a specific design or business decision—if not, dig deeper or stop.

## Evaluation rubrics

### Two Cycle Diverge Converge
- **When**: Full product development from problem to solution.
- **Process**: Discover (diverge on problem)  ->  define (converge on problem statement)  ->  develop (diverge on solutions)  ->  deliver (converge on shipped product). Iterate as needed.
- **Pass**: Problem validated before solution commitment; solution tested before ship.
- **Failure signals**: Skipping problem definition; linear waterfall without iteration.

## Anti-patterns

- **Jump to solution**: First idea built without observation.
- **Single brainstorm**: Ideation without subsequent prototype/test.
- **Fake iteration**: Testing only to validate, not to learn.
- **Requirements from asking users**: "What do you need?" yields surface problems.
- **Stopping when problem statement satisfied**: Missing fundamental issues.
- **Throwing designs over the wall**: Interaction design finished before engineering involvement.
- **Design by committee**: Consensus-driven feature accumulation without persona focus.
- **Extended team as approvers only**: Stakeholders who gate without participating in discovery.

## Examples

- **Creative Teamwork**: A design studio session has interaction designers, visual designers, and developers sketch framework alternatives for mobile banking in 90 minutes. Each produces six concepts; the group evaluates against the primary persona's goal: check whether I can afford this purchase without anxiety. The winning direction merges a developer's real-time balance insight with a glanceable status indicator. The team presents findings in a debrief with video clips and a prioritized issue list—assigned owners leave the room with fixes scheduled for the next sprint, not a PDF archived in email.
