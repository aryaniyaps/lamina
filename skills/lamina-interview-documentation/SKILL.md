# Interview Documentation

## Decision frameworks

- **Note-Taking Approaches**: Real-time notes, post-session expansion, split moderator/notetaker roles. - Use during every session. - How: Notetaker captures quotes and observations; moderator focuses on facilitation.
- **Recording (Audio/Video)**: Necessary for accuracy; requires consent and backup equipment. - Use when analysis needs verbatim review. - How: Test levels before session; redundant device; explicit consent on record.
- **Photography and Artifact Collection**: Environment, tools, forms, screens—with permission. - Use in contextual fieldwork. - How: Ask before shooting; capture artifacts participants reference.
- **Limitations of Notes**: Incomplete, biased toward what notetaker noticed; unique strength in selective emphasis. - Use when deciding capture strategy. - How: Don't rely on notes alone for quotes; triangulate with recording.
- **Transcription**: Full transcript enables team analysis but is time-intensive. - Use for high-stakes or multi-analyst studies. - How: Budget time and cost; consider selective transcription of key sessions.
- **Transcription for Team Analysis**: Full or selective transcripts enable rigorous multi-analyst synthesis. - Use when findings will be contested or widely shared. - How: Budget time and cost; transcribe key sessions first; timestamp for video sync.
- **Topline Summary**: Immediate post-field highlights before deep analysis—what surprised, what patterns emerging. - Use within 24 hours of last session. - How: Bullet key themes; share with team before memory fades.
- **Affinity Diagramming**: Cluster observations on sticky notes to reveal patterns organically. - Use during team synthesis workshops. - How: One observation per note; silent sort; group and label clusters.
- **Reading Transcripts and Identifying Patterns/Themes**: Systematic review for recurring behaviors, workarounds, and unmet needs. - Use when recordings/transcripts available. - How: Color-code; tag with research questions; look for repetition across participants.
- **Delivering Results**: Formats matched to audience—executive summary, highlight reel, workshop, not report-only. - Use when sharing findings. - How: Lead with implications; support with evidence; prioritize actionable items.
- **Team Involvement in Analysis**: Stakeholders who participate in synthesis own findings more deeply. - Use when building organizational buy-in. - How: Invite to affinity sessions; have them read transcripts; co-create implications.
- **Team Involvement in Analysis**: Stakeholders who cluster stickies and read transcripts own findings more deeply. - Use for every significant study. - How: Half-day affinity workshop; invite skeptics; co-write implications.

## Checklists

1. Split facilitation and note-taking; never one person doing both.
2. Record sessions with explicit consent and backup equipment.
3. Notes and recordings complement each other—neither alone suffices.
4. Capture artifacts and environment photos when permitted.
5. Document for synthesis needs—budget transcription and analysis time upfront.
1. Topline summaries capture immediate patterns; deep synthesis validates them.
2. Affinity diagramming reveals patterns through team clustering of observations.
3. Involve stakeholders in analysis—they own what they help create.
4. Go beyond summarizing quotes—interpret behavior and implications.
5. Match deliverable format to audience decision needs.

## Heuristics

- **Proper recording is necessity**: Not luxury—for accurate quotes and team review.
- **Notes capture what recording misses**: Context, body language emphasis, researcher impressions.
- **PII and privacy**: Handle recordings and photos per consent and legal requirements.
- **Immediate capture**: Index cards and debrief notes before memory fades.
- **Documentation for synthesis**: Capture serves analysis, not archive for its own sake.
- Notes are**highlights reel**; recording is**full game tape**—need both.
- Documentation starts**during**the session, not after.
- Every artifact collected is**evidence for synthesis**, not souvenir.
- **Decision filter**: Ask whether this finding changes a specific design or business decision—if not, dig deeper or stop.
- **Saturation assessment**: Patterns stable across participants—or need more interviews.
- **"We didn't hear anything new"**: Symptom of shallow analysis or misaligned objectives.
- **Beyond summarizing what people told you**: Interpret behavior and implications.
- **Topline vs. deep synthesis**: Speed for alignment; depth for design decisions.
- **If customers repeat same needs, ask why haven't we acted**: Research accountability.
- Synthesis is**compression with fidelity**—patterns, not anecdotes.
- Affinity diagramming is**collaborative sense-making**, not solo categorization.
- Findings without**implications**are trivia. - **Decision filter**: Ask whether this finding changes a specific design or business decision—if not, dig deeper or stop.

## Anti-patterns

- **Moderator-only notes**: Split attention degrades facilitation and capture.
- **No recording because "I take good notes"**: Misquoted users in findings.
- **Photos without permission**: Trust violation and legal risk.
- **Transcripts without analysis plan**: Expensive documentation nobody reads.
- **Report-first synthesis**: Writing before clustering and team review.
- **Cherry-picked quotes**: Supporting predetermined conclusions.
- **Solo analyst ivory tower**: Findings nobody else trusts or understands.
- **Stopping at topline**: Sharing highlights without rigorous pattern validation.

## Examples

- **Making Sense Of Data**: After eight interviews, facilitates affinity workshop: team writes 120 observation stickies from notes and transcripts, silently clusters them into "Workarounds," "Trust barriers," and "Success moments." Theme "Users rebuild reports in Excel because export is broken" appears in six of eight sessions—actionable insight. Topline emailed day after fieldwork flagged same theme for stakeholder preview. Deliverable: 10-minute highlight reel plus prioritized recommendation list, not 60-page report.
